# KHALAS

## Purpose
`KHALAS.md` is the repository's cumulative thesis ledger. It states the current repository-level case that the Prophet ﷺ fulfills the biblical prophetic expectation, while preserving the distinction between anchored passage findings, cross-passage synthesis, project-level inference, speculative resonance, and controls.

This file does not cite, quote, or reproduce working corpus files. It links only to repository passage pages and argument clusters.

## Source-Status Legend
- `[anchored]`: directly tied to a promoted passage page or argument page.
- `[synthesis]`: produced by joining multiple promoted pages or clusters.
- `[inference]`: a project-level conclusion drawn from the cumulative structure.
- `[speculative]`: too thin to govern the argument; retained only as review or resonance.
- `[control]`: used to test alternatives, constraints, or failure modes rather than to prove the thesis.

## Thesis Spine
The primary spine is not fourteen equal arguments. It is a ranked route from direct prophetic profile to candidate-field narrowing, then to expectation context and corroboration.

1. [Prophet Like Moses](arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md) supplies the Torah-side direct profile: a raised prophet, likeness to Moses, received words, and accountability for response.
2. [Paraclete](arguments/paraclete/summary.md) supplies the Gospel-side direct profile: a post-Jesus guide/witness whose speech is received rather than autonomous.
3. [Arabian Servant And Kedar](arguments/arabian-servant-and-kedar/summary.md), [Ishmaelite Line And Brethren](arguments/ishmaelite-line-and-brethren/summary.md), and [Paran And Arabia](arguments/paran-and-arabia/summary.md) narrow the candidate field toward law-to-nations, Kedar, Ishmaelite, and Arabian geography.
4. [Messenger Before Arrival](arguments/messenger-before-arrival/summary.md), [Live Prophetic Expectation](arguments/live-prophetic-expectation/summary.md), and [Prophet Like Moses And Paraclete](arguments/prophet-like-moses-and-paraclete/summary.md) explain why the expectation remains a live interpretive question, while preventing dependent syntheses from being double-counted.
5. [Unlettered Prophet](arguments/unlettered-prophet/summary.md), [Kingdom Transfer](arguments/kingdom-transfer/summary.md), [Baca And Pilgrimage](arguments/baca-and-pilgrimage/summary.md), and [Mahmad And Praised-One](arguments/mahmad-and-praised-one/summary.md) add secondary or corroborative fit only after the primary spine survives.

A primary failure in Deuteronomy 18 or the Paraclete cluster would materially weaken the thesis. A failure in Baca, Mahmad, Isaiah 41, or other tentative material would not defeat the thesis because those lines are not load-bearing.

## Primary Load-Bearing Clusters
### Prophet Like Moses
[anchored] [Deuteronomy 18:15-22](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v15-22/summary.md) establishes the main Torah-side prophetic expectation: a prophet raised by God, compared to Moses, speaking received words, and requiring response. [synthesis] [Deuteronomy 34:10](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch34/v10/summary.md), [John 1:19-21](bible/nt/john/ch1/v19-21/summary.md), and [Acts 3:22-23](bible/nt/acts/ch3/v22-23/summary.md) make the cluster reviewable rather than simple: the expectation remains live, but Acts 3 also supplies the strongest Christian counter-reading. This cluster is primary because it gives the clearest office-profile and speech-profile foundation.

Its pressure on the thesis is that the expected figure is not merely “inspired,” but publicly authorized to deliver received divine speech with Moses-like consequence. It would fail as a primary cluster if Deuteronomy 18 is fully exhausted by Israelite prophetic succession or by the Acts 3 Jesus reading, leaving no unresolved prophet-like-Moses expectation for the cumulative case.

### Paraclete
[anchored] [John 14:16-17](bible/nt/john/ch14/v16-17/summary.md), [John 14:26](bible/nt/john/ch14/v26/summary.md), [John 15:26](bible/nt/john/ch15/v26/summary.md), and [John 16:7-13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v7-13/summary.md) form the main Gospel-side promise cluster. [synthesis] The argument turns on departure, later guidance, witness, received speech, and future disclosure, especially through [John 16:13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v13/summary.md). It is primary but objection-sensitive because the Holy Spirit/Pentecost reading remains the dominant Christian synthesis and must be tested through [John 7:39](bible/nt/john/ch7/v39/summary.md), [John 20:22](bible/nt/john/ch20/v22/summary.md), [Luke 24:49](bible/nt/luke/ch24/v49/summary.md), and [1 John 4:1-6](bible/nt/1-john/ch4/v1-6/summary.md).

Its pressure on the thesis is the convergence of post-departure timing, guidance, witness to Jesus, future disclosure, and non-autonomous speech. It would fail as a primary cluster if the Johannine Spirit/Pentecost reading accounts for those features without remainder, or if “another” and “he will speak what he hears” cannot bear any human-messenger synthesis.

## Secondary Candidate-Field Support
These clusters matter because they answer the candidate question left open by the primary profile. They are not independent proof engines; they narrow the field by lineage, geography, Kedar, law-to-nations, and Arabian setting.

### Arabian Servant And Kedar
[anchored] [Isaiah 42:1-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v1-17/summary.md) supplies servant, justice, law-to-nations, anti-idolatry, and victory language. [synthesis] [Isaiah 42:11](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v11/summary.md), [Genesis 25:13](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v13/summary.md), [Ezekiel 27:21](bible/ot/ezekiel/ch27/v21/summary.md), and [Isaiah 21:13-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch21/v13-17/summary.md) connect Kedar/Sela and Arabia to the candidate field. Its strength is medium-high because the servant-cycle, Israel, and Jesus readings remain serious.

### Ishmaelite Line And Brethren
[anchored] [Genesis 16:12](bible/ot/genesis/ch16/v12/summary.md), [Genesis 17:20](bible/ot/genesis/ch17/v20/summary.md), [Genesis 21:13-21](bible/ot/genesis/ch21/v13-21/summary.md), [Genesis 25:13](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v13/summary.md), and [Genesis 25:18](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v18/summary.md) establish an Ishmael/Kedar line and geography. [synthesis] This supports the “brethren” question in Deuteronomy 18, but does not settle it by itself because an Israelite-only reading of “brethren” remains live.

### Paran And Arabia
[anchored] [Deuteronomy 33:1-2](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch33/v1-2/summary.md), [Habakkuk 3:3](bible/ot/habakkuk/ch3/v3/summary.md), [Isaiah 21:13-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch21/v13-17/summary.md), and [Ezekiel 27:21](bible/ot/ezekiel/ch27/v21/summary.md) create a repeated geography field. [synthesis] The field becomes meaningful only when joined to the primary prophet profile and Isaiah 42/Kedar material; otherwise it risks becoming a place-name argument. Divine-warrior theophany and local ancient geography remain the main constraints.

## Expectation Context And Dependent Synthesis
[Messenger Before Arrival](arguments/messenger-before-arrival/summary.md) routes [Malachi 3:1-4](bible/ot/malachi/ch3/v1-4/summary.md) and [Malachi 4:4-6](bible/ot/malachi/ch4/v4-6/summary.md) as expectation material, but it must keep the John-the-Baptist/Jesus reading visible through [Mark 1:2-3](bible/nt/mark/ch1/v2-3/summary.md), [Luke 7:26-28](bible/nt/luke/ch7/v26-28/summary.md), [Matthew 11:11-15](bible/nt/matthew/ch11/v11-15/summary.md), and [Matthew 17:10-13](bible/nt/matthew/ch17/v10-13/summary.md).

[Live Prophetic Expectation](arguments/live-prophetic-expectation/summary.md) gathers [John 1:19-21](bible/nt/john/ch1/v19-21/summary.md), [John 4:25](bible/nt/john/ch4/v25/summary.md), [Luke 24:19](bible/nt/luke/ch24/v19/summary.md), [Acts 1:6](bible/nt/acts/ch1/v6/summary.md), and [Acts 5:34-39](bible/nt/acts/ch5/v34-39/summary.md) to show that expectation categories were active. It does not identify the Prophet ﷺ; it supplies context.

[Prophet Like Moses And Paraclete](arguments/prophet-like-moses-and-paraclete/summary.md) is useful because [Deuteronomy 18:18](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v18/summary.md) and [John 16:13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v13/summary.md) share received-speech logic. It is dependent synthesis and must not be counted as a third independent primary proof.

## Current Cumulative Judgment
[anchored] The repository now contains promoted passage pages for the primary direct clusters, the secondary candidate-field clusters, the expectation/context clusters, and the main control sets. [synthesis] The current strongest thesis spine is: Deuteronomy 18 supplies the prophetic office and received-speech profile; John 14-16 supplies a later guide/witness profile; Isaiah/Kedar, Ishmael, and Paran/Arabia narrow the candidate field; expectation and control clusters test whether this reading survives rival Jewish and Christian syntheses. [inference] The present repository-level conclusion is that the Prophet ﷺ fulfills the biblical prophetic expectation through disciplined cumulative convergence, provided the primary clusters remain standing and the secondary clusters are kept in proper rank.

The strongest unresolved tension is whether the two primary clusters remain genuinely open after their strongest internal readings are applied: Acts 3 presses Deuteronomy 18 toward Jesus, while Johannine Spirit/Pentecost controls press the Paraclete toward the Holy Spirit. The cumulative thesis depends on showing that those readings do not exhaust the received-speech, later-expectation, and candidate-field convergence when the Bible spine is read across clusters.

## Failure Modes To Avoid
- Do not let lexical resemblance substitute for convergence.
- Do not cite a single place-name as if it proves the full case.
- Do not hide the Holy Spirit reading of the Paraclete.
- Do not treat Acts 3, Gospel Malachi uses, or John-the-Baptist readings as irrelevant.
- Do not promote broader Christology or law-control material as if it were direct prophecy.
- Do not double-count dependent synthesis as independent evidence.
- Do not quote or cite working corpus files in public-facing pages.
