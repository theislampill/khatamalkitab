This is the project's current findings ledger. It does not repeat raw corpus text. It records what the repository currently treats as the main findings after normalization, passage classification, and convergence review.

## Status Legend
- `High-convergence`: central finding with strong corpus recurrence and stable argumentative role
- `Medium-convergence`: real finding, but more dependent on supporting chains or thinner recurrence
- `Control`: not a primary prophecy claim; used to test or pressure competing readings
- `Tentative`: present in the corpus but still too unstable to carry much project weight

## All Together
This section gives the constructive synthesis that the rest of the repository is built to support: showing the Prophet ﷺ in the Bible through cumulative prophetic fulfillment rather than isolated proof-texting.

The synthesis applies the method in [METHOD.md](METHOD.md) and the local daee-epistemics discipline of diagnosis, convergence, proof-method auditing, and inference-boundary marking. The corpus has been used here only for discovery and routing pressure. The reader-facing argument below cites repository passage pages and argument clusters, not the working corpus.

### Constructive Argument
`[anchored]` The core expectation begins with the prophet-like-Moses promise in [Deuteronomy 18:15-22](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v15-22/summary.md). The strongest individual verse is [Deuteronomy 18:18](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v18/summary.md): the promised prophet is like Moses, comes from the brethren, receives God's words in his mouth, and speaks what he is commanded. [Deuteronomy 18:19](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v19/summary.md) makes response to him religiously consequential, while [Deuteronomy 18:21](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v21/summary.md) and [Deuteronomy 18:22](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v22/summary.md) keep the claim accountable to prophetic truth-testing.

`[synthesis]` The New Testament does not close this expectation inside Jesus in a simple way. [John 1:19-21](bible/nt/john/ch1/v19-21/summary.md) distinguishes the Messiah, Elijah, and "the Prophet," while [Acts 3:22-23](bible/nt/acts/ch3/v22-23/summary.md) continues to invoke Moses' promised prophet. This makes the Deuteronomy line a live expectation rather than a disconnected Old Testament proof text. The governing cluster is [Prophet Like Moses](arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md).

`[anchored]` The "brethren" line has a biblical route through Ishmael. [Genesis 17:20](bible/ot/genesis/ch17/v20/summary.md) blesses Ishmael with nationhood, [Genesis 21:13-21](bible/ot/genesis/ch21/v13-21/summary.md) develops the great-nation and Paran line, [Genesis 16:12](bible/ot/genesis/ch16/v12/summary.md) and [Genesis 25:18](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v18/summary.md) preserve the brethren language, and [Genesis 25:13](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v13/summary.md) identifies Kedar among Ishmael's sons. The governing cluster is [Ishmaelite Line and Brethren](arguments/ishmaelite-line-and-brethren/summary.md).

`[synthesis]` The Arabian route is not carried by one place-name alone. [Deuteronomy 33:1-2](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch33/v1-2/summary.md), [Habakkuk 3:3](bible/ot/habakkuk/ch3/v3/summary.md), [Isaiah 21:13-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch21/v13-17/summary.md), and [Ezekiel 27:21](bible/ot/ezekiel/ch27/v21/summary.md) place Paran, Teman, Arabia, Tema, Dedan, and Kedar into a mutually reinforcing geography. The governing cluster is [Paran and Arabia](arguments/paran-and-arabia/summary.md).

`[anchored]` [Isaiah 42:1-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v1-17/summary.md) strengthens the profile by adding servant, nations, law, victory, and Kedar. [Isaiah 42:4](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v4/summary.md) gives the law/judgment-to-the-nations line, [Isaiah 42:6](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v6/summary.md) expands the servant's scope, [Isaiah 42:11](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v11/summary.md) names Kedar's settlements, and [Isaiah 42:13](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v13/summary.md) introduces victorious struggle. The governing cluster is [Arabian Servant and Kedar](arguments/arabian-servant-and-kedar/summary.md).

`[anchored]` The Johannine Paraclete line gives the New Testament form of the promised successor argument. [John 14:16](bible/nt/john/ch14/v16/summary.md) promises another advocate, [John 14:26](bible/nt/john/ch14/v26/summary.md) gives teaching and reminder functions, [John 15:26](bible/nt/john/ch15/v26/summary.md) gives witness to Jesus, [John 16:7](bible/nt/john/ch16/v7/summary.md) ties his coming to Jesus' departure, and [John 16:13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v13/summary.md) gives the decisive mediated-speech pattern: he does not speak from himself but speaks what he hears and announces what is to come. The governing cluster is [Paraclete](arguments/paraclete/summary.md).

`[synthesis]` The Paraclete line converges with Deuteronomy 18 because both profiles center on mediated revelation: words placed in the mouth in [Deuteronomy 18:18](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v18/summary.md), and speech received rather than self-originated in [John 16:13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v13/summary.md). The same Johannine passage adds functions that fit the Prophet ﷺ as a later witness to Jesus: conviction in [John 16:8](bible/nt/john/ch16/v8/summary.md), glorification of Jesus in [John 16:14](bible/nt/john/ch16/v14/summary.md), and reception/declaration in [John 16:15](bible/nt/john/ch16/v15/summary.md).

`[anchored]` The Paraclete reading is not left uncontrolled. [John 7:39](bible/nt/john/ch7/v39/summary.md), [John 20:22](bible/nt/john/ch20/v22/summary.md), [Luke 24:49](bible/nt/luke/ch24/v49/summary.md), and [1 John 4:1-6](bible/nt/1-john/ch4/v1-6/summary.md) are used to test the Spirit-reading and prophet-reading. [John 14:28](bible/nt/john/ch14/v28/summary.md) and [John 14:30-31](bible/nt/john/ch14/v30-31/summary.md) add departure and subordination pressure. The governing control cluster is [Paraclete and Subordination](arguments/paraclete-and-subordination/summary.md).

`[synthesis]` The supporting clusters do not replace the core case; they increase convergence. [Unlettered Prophet](arguments/unlettered-prophet/summary.md) joins [Isaiah 28:11-12](bible/ot/isaiah/ch28/v11-12/summary.md) and [Isaiah 29:11-12](bible/ot/isaiah/ch29/v11-12/summary.md). [Baca and Pilgrimage](arguments/baca-and-pilgrimage/summary.md) joins [Psalm 84:6](bible/ot/psalms/ch84/v6/summary.md) to pilgrimage geography. [Mahmad and Praised-One](arguments/mahmad-and-praised-one/summary.md) joins [Haggai 2:7](bible/ot/haggai/ch2/v7/summary.md) and [Song of Songs 5:16](bible/ot/song-of-songs/ch5/v16/summary.md). [Kingdom Transfer](arguments/kingdom-transfer/summary.md) joins [Genesis 49:10](bible/ot/genesis/ch49/v10/summary.md), [Daniel 2:44](bible/ot/daniel/ch2/v44/summary.md), and [Matthew 21:42-44](bible/nt/matthew/ch21/v42-44/summary.md).

### Why The Case Is Cumulative
`[synthesis]` The case for the Prophet ﷺ as biblical prophetic fulfillment does not depend on one lexical coincidence, one disputed manuscript claim, one isolated place-name, or one New Testament passage read in isolation. The fulfillment profile joins office, lineage, geography, timing, law, mediated revelation, witness to Jesus, judgment against idolatry, historical reception, and public religious consequence.

`[synthesis]` The strongest load-bearing lines are [Prophet Like Moses](arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md), [Paraclete](arguments/paraclete/summary.md), [Arabian Servant and Kedar](arguments/arabian-servant-and-kedar/summary.md), [Paran and Arabia](arguments/paran-and-arabia/summary.md), and [Ishmaelite Line and Brethren](arguments/ishmaelite-line-and-brethren/summary.md). The corroborating lines are [Unlettered Prophet](arguments/unlettered-prophet/summary.md), [Baca and Pilgrimage](arguments/baca-and-pilgrimage/summary.md), [Mahmad and Praised-One](arguments/mahmad-and-praised-one/summary.md), and [Kingdom Transfer](arguments/kingdom-transfer/summary.md).

`[synthesis]` A critic may weaken an individual support line without defeating the whole case. A serious objection must explain why the Moses-like prophet, the still-live expectation, the Ishmaelite brethren line, the Arabian/Kedar geography, the servant-to-nations profile, and the Johannine promised-guide profile do not converge.

### Failure Modes This Avoids
- It does not force every verse to carry the whole thesis.
- It does not treat supporting lexical material as equal to the main prophecy clusters.
- It does not hide objections; control passages remain part of the argument.
- It does not quote-dump working corpus material.
- It does not depend on the reader accepting one isolated English rendering.

### Bottom Line
`[inference]` The repository's constructive demonstration is cumulative and decisive: the Prophet ﷺ fulfills the biblical expectation when the promoted passages are read together. He fits the prophet-like-Moses office, the Ishmaelite-brethren route, the Arabian/Kedar geography, the servant-to-nations profile, the promised post-Jesus guide profile, and the corroborating signs of recitation, pilgrimage, praised-name language, and kingdom transfer. Individual pages keep the claims auditable; combined pages preserve local context; argument clusters show why the whole case is stronger than the sum of isolated proof texts.
