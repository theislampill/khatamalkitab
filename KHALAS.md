# KHALAS

## Purpose
`KHALAS.md` is the repository's cumulative thesis ledger. It states the current repository-level case that the Prophet ﷺ fulfills the biblical prophetic expectation, while preserving the distinction between anchored passage findings, cross-passage synthesis, project-level inference, speculative resonance, and controls.

This file does not cite, quote, or reproduce working corpus files. It links only to repository passage pages and argument clusters.

## Source-Status Legend
- `[anchored]`: directly tied to a promoted passage page or argument page.
- `[synthesis]`: produced by joining multiple promoted pages or clusters.
- `[inference]`: a project-level conclusion drawn from the cumulative structure.
- `[speculative]`: too thin to govern the argument; retained only as review or resonance.
- `[control]`: used to test alternatives, constraints, or failure modes rather than to prove the thesis.

## Thesis Spine
The primary spine is not fourteen equal arguments. It is a ranked route from direct prophetic profile to candidate-field narrowing, then to expectation context and corroboration.

1. [Prophet Like Moses](arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md) supplies the Torah-side direct profile: a raised prophet, likeness to Moses, received words, and accountability for response.
2. [Paraclete](arguments/paraclete/summary.md) supplies the Gospel-side direct profile: a post-Jesus guide/witness whose speech is received rather than autonomous.
3. [Arabian Servant And Kedar](arguments/arabian-servant-and-kedar/summary.md), [Ishmaelite Line And Brethren](arguments/ishmaelite-line-and-brethren/summary.md), and [Paran And Arabia](arguments/paran-and-arabia/summary.md) narrow the candidate field toward law-to-nations, Kedar, Ishmaelite, and Arabian geography.
4. [Messenger Before Arrival](arguments/messenger-before-arrival/summary.md) and [Live Prophetic Expectation](arguments/live-prophetic-expectation/summary.md) explain why the expectation remains a live interpretive question rather than a closed one. [Prophet Like Moses And Paraclete](arguments/prophet-like-moses-and-paraclete/summary.md) then tests cross-canon coherence at the dependent-synthesis layer and must not be double-counted as a fresh witness.
5. [Unlettered Prophet](arguments/unlettered-prophet/summary.md), [Kingdom Transfer](arguments/kingdom-transfer/summary.md), [Baca And Pilgrimage](arguments/baca-and-pilgrimage/summary.md), and [Mahmad And Praised-One](arguments/mahmad-and-praised-one/summary.md) add secondary or corroborative fit only after the primary spine survives.

That order matters. The repository is strongest when it moves from direct profile, to candidate-field narrowing, to expectation context, and only then to corroboration. Read in that sequence, the argument does not ask any single verse to identify the Prophet ﷺ by itself, and it does not ask weaker material to carry what the primary clusters must carry for themselves.

A primary failure in Deuteronomy 18 or the Paraclete cluster would materially weaken the thesis. A failure in Baca, Mahmad, Isaiah 41, or other tentative material would not defeat the thesis because those lines are not load-bearing.

## Primary Load-Bearing Clusters
### Prophet Like Moses
[anchored] [Deuteronomy 18:15-22](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v15-22/summary.md) establishes the main Torah-side prophetic expectation: a prophet raised by God, compared to Moses, speaking received words, and requiring response. [synthesis] [Deuteronomy 34:10](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch34/v10/summary.md), [John 1:19-21](bible/nt/john/ch1/v19-21/summary.md), [Acts 3:21](bible/nt/acts/ch3/v21/summary.md), and [Acts 3:22-23](bible/nt/acts/ch3/v22-23/summary.md) make the cluster reviewable rather than simple: the expectation remains live, but Acts 3 also supplies the strongest Christian counter-reading inside a future-restoration frame. This cluster is primary because it gives the clearest office-profile and speech-profile foundation.

Its pressure on the thesis is that the expected figure is not merely “inspired,” but publicly authorized to deliver received divine speech with Moses-like consequence. It would fail as a primary cluster if Deuteronomy 18 is fully exhausted by Israelite prophetic succession or by the Acts 3 Jesus reading, leaving no unresolved prophet-like-Moses expectation for the cumulative case.

### Paraclete
[anchored] [John 14:16-17](bible/nt/john/ch14/v16-17/summary.md), [John 14:26](bible/nt/john/ch14/v26/summary.md), [John 15:26](bible/nt/john/ch15/v26/summary.md), and [John 16:7-13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v7-13/summary.md) form the main Gospel-side promise cluster. [synthesis] The argument turns on departure-conditioned coming, world-facing conviction, later guidance, witness, received speech, and future disclosure, especially through [John 16:8](bible/nt/john/ch16/v8/summary.md), [John 16:12](bible/nt/john/ch16/v12/summary.md), and [John 16:13](bible/nt/john/ch16/v13/summary.md). [John 12:49](bible/nt/john/ch12/v49/summary.md) matters as an internal Johannine speech-profile control: non-self-originated speech is not alien to John's representation of authoritative revelation. It is primary but objection-sensitive because the Holy Spirit/Pentecost reading remains the dominant Christian synthesis and must be tested through [Acts 2:1-4](bible/nt/acts/ch2/v1-4/summary.md), [John 7:39](bible/nt/john/ch7/v39/summary.md), [John 20:22](bible/nt/john/ch20/v22/summary.md), [Luke 24:49](bible/nt/luke/ch24/v49/summary.md), and [1 John 4:1-6](bible/nt/1-john/ch4/v1-6/summary.md).

Its pressure on the thesis is the convergence of post-departure timing, guidance, witness to Jesus, future disclosure, and non-autonomous speech. It would fail as a primary cluster if the Johannine Spirit/Pentecost reading accounts for those features without remainder, or if “another” and “he will speak what he hears” cannot bear any human-messenger synthesis.

## Secondary Candidate-Field Support
These clusters matter because they answer the candidate question left open by the primary profile. They are not independent proof engines. Together they form one braided narrowing complex: Isaiah 42/Kedar adds mission-profile plus Kedar-facing contact, Ishmael/brethren adds lineage eligibility, and Paran/Arabia adds regional mapping. Their force lies in differentiated constraints pointing toward the same field, not in multiplying witness count.

### Arabian Servant And Kedar
[anchored] [Isaiah 42:1-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v1-17/summary.md) supplies servant, justice, law-to-nations, anti-idolatry, and victory language. [synthesis] [Isaiah 42:11](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v11/summary.md), [Genesis 25:13](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v13/summary.md), [Ezekiel 27:21](bible/ot/ezekiel/ch27/v21/summary.md), [Isaiah 21:13-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch21/v13-17/summary.md), and [Isaiah 60:6-7](bible/ot/isaiah/ch60/v6-7/summary.md) connect that mission-profile to Kedar/Sela, Arabia, and secondary sanctuary/Kedar motifs. This cluster adds strong secondary mission-and-geography pressure, not direct identification by itself. Its strength is medium-high because the servant-cycle, Israel, and Jesus readings remain serious.

### Ishmaelite Line And Brethren
[anchored] [Genesis 16:12](bible/ot/genesis/ch16/v12/summary.md), [Genesis 17:20](bible/ot/genesis/ch17/v20/summary.md), [Genesis 21:13-21](bible/ot/genesis/ch21/v13-21/summary.md), [Genesis 25:13](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v13/summary.md), and [Genesis 25:18](bible/ot/genesis/ch25/v18/summary.md) establish an Ishmael/Kedar line and geography. [synthesis] This cluster contributes lineage eligibility and “brethren” pressure to Deuteronomy 18, but it does not settle the question by itself because an Israelite-only reading of “brethren” remains live.

### Paran And Arabia
[anchored] [Genesis 21:13-21](bible/ot/genesis/ch21/v13-21/summary.md), [Deuteronomy 33:1-2](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch33/v1-2/summary.md), [Habakkuk 3:3](bible/ot/habakkuk/ch3/v3/summary.md), and [Isaiah 21:13-17](bible/ot/isaiah/ch21/v13-17/summary.md) supply the core place-name data. [synthesis] [Ezekiel 27:21](bible/ot/ezekiel/ch27/v21/summary.md) and [Isaiah 42:11](bible/ot/isaiah/ch42/v11/summary.md) bridge Kedar into that wider regional field. Read together, they create a repeated geography field and regional map constraint. That field becomes meaningful only when joined to the primary prophet profile and servant/Kedar material; otherwise it risks becoming a place-name argument. Divine-warrior theophany and local ancient geography remain the main constraints.

## Current Cumulative Judgment
[synthesis] The repository now contains promoted passage pages for the primary direct clusters, the secondary candidate-field clusters, the expectation/context clusters, and the main control sets. Taken together, they do more than catalogue possible prophecies. They create a ranked argument in which office-profile, speech-profile, timing, candidate-field narrowing, and expectation context reinforce one another without being collapsed into a single proof text. [inference] The present repository-level conclusion remains affirmative but disciplined: the Prophet ﷺ is the best cumulative fit to the biblical prophetic expectation mapped here, provided the primary clusters remain standing and the secondary clusters are kept in proper rank.

The strongest unresolved tension is whether the two primary clusters remain genuinely open after their strongest internal readings are applied: Acts 3 presses Deuteronomy 18 toward Jesus, while Johannine Spirit/Pentecost controls press the Paraclete toward the Holy Spirit. If those readings are exhaustive, the cumulative case collapses at its foundation. If they are not exhaustive, the secondary clusters become materially important because they help identify where the surviving profile most plausibly lands.

The constructive argument therefore has to answer four questions in order: what kind of figure must still be found, whether John preserves a second unresolved profile or closes it in the Spirit, where a surviving candidate field points, and why that movement is not merely retrospective stitching.

### Constructive Argument
[anchored] The case begins with [Prophet Like Moses](arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md), because Deuteronomy 18 gives the clearest direct profile in the repository: not a loose prophecy theme, but a singled-out obedience-bearing prophet horizon. God raises the figure, the figure is like Moses, the figure speaks received divine words, and refusal of that speech has covenantal consequence. [Deuteronomy 18:16-17](bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v16/summary.md) matters here because the promise answers Israel's Horeb request for mediated revelation, so the cluster is about authorized mediation rather than generic inspiration. That does not yet identify the Prophet ﷺ, but it establishes the kind of claimant the thesis must find. This is why the cluster remains load-bearing even with [Acts 3:22-23](bible/nt/acts/ch3/v22-23/summary.md) in view: the argument cannot proceed unless the Moses-like expectation still has meaningful room after both its strongest Deuteronomic reading and its strongest Christian application are considered.

[anchored] The next question is whether the Gospel side preserves only an already-closed Spirit promise, or whether a second unresolved profile remains. [Paraclete](arguments/paraclete/summary.md) is not a duplicate of Deuteronomy 18 but a second direct profile from a different location in the canon. John 14-16 adds departure-conditioned coming, world-facing conviction, witness to Jesus, deferred and future disclosure, and received rather than autonomous speech. [John 12:49](bible/nt/john/ch12/v49/summary.md) makes the received-speech predicate internally Johannine before it is compared with Deuteronomy 18. The question here is not whether the Johannine material is uncontested; it is whether John's Spirit-of-truth / Holy Spirit / indwelling-disciple frame, together with [Acts 2:1-4](bible/nt/acts/ch2/v1-4/summary.md), fully absorbs that sequence. Any later-figure reading is excluded immediately if it severs witness to Jesus, because the cluster's own terms require glorifying him rather than negating him. If Pentecost is a real Spirit-fulfillment event but not an exhaustive one, then a residual later-guidance and witness profile remains to be explained. Only at the synthesis layer does the repository ask whether that remainder stands in notable continuity with the Torah-side received-speech pattern.

[synthesis] If both primary profiles survive their controls, the next question is where the candidate field actually points. The secondary clusters do that next piece of real argumentative work. [Arabian Servant And Kedar](arguments/arabian-servant-and-kedar/summary.md), [Ishmaelite Line And Brethren](arguments/ishmaelite-line-and-brethren/summary.md), and [Paran And Arabia](arguments/paran-and-arabia/summary.md) do not prove the thesis by lineage or geography alone, and they should not be read as three separate votes. They function as one braided narrowing complex. Isaiah 42/Kedar contributes mission-profile plus Kedar-facing contact; Ishmael/brethren contributes lineage eligibility; Paran/Arabia contributes regional mapping. Their force is narrowing by non-identical constraints. The pressure is not merely that Arabia appears. It is that Arabia appears in conjunction with Kedar/Ishmael and with a servant profile marked by law-to-nations, anti-idolatry contrast, and public victory. [Matthew 15:24](bible/nt/matthew/ch15/v24/summary.md) and [John 18:36](bible/nt/john/ch18/v36/summary.md) keep the Jesus reading serious, but they do not straightforwardly absorb that full conjunction as currently routed. This does not erase the rival reading that Kedar and Sela may describe the praising horizon of the nations rather than the servant's origin; it states only that, if the servant cluster is not exhausted that way, its geography still participates in narrowing the field. This is the point at which the case stops looking like an abstract prophecy discussion and starts becoming historically directional.

[control] The next question is whether this movement is only retrospective stitching. The expectation and context clusters answer that narrower challenge. [Messenger Before Arrival](arguments/messenger-before-arrival/summary.md) and [Live Prophetic Expectation](arguments/live-prophetic-expectation/summary.md) show that prophetic and messenger categories remained active, differentiated, and contested. [Acts 1:6](bible/nt/acts/ch1/v6/summary.md) and [Acts 5:34-39](bible/nt/acts/ch5/v34-39/summary.md) matter here because restoration hope and divine-validation caution both resist premature declarations that the field is already closed. These clusters do not identify the Prophet ﷺ on their own, and their main role is anti-closure: they show why the reader is not required to treat every major expectation as already closed before the argument even begins. Malachi in particular remains expectation context under strong inner-canonical closure, not a comparably open primary field. [Prophet Like Moses And Paraclete](arguments/prophet-like-moses-and-paraclete/summary.md) then clarifies a coherence claim about received speech across the two primary clusters, while remaining explicitly dependent so it does not get counted as a third primary proof.

[inference] Only after that structure is in place do the corroborative lines become useful. [Unlettered Prophet](arguments/unlettered-prophet/summary.md), [Kingdom Transfer](arguments/kingdom-transfer/summary.md), [Baca And Pilgrimage](arguments/baca-and-pilgrimage/summary.md), and [Mahmad And Praised-One](arguments/mahmad-and-praised-one/summary.md) can widen the fit, intensify the cumulative impression, or explain why the argument has had enduring persuasive force in the Muslim reading of the Bible. They are not the foundation, and they do not add witness count. Their legitimate role is confirmatory asymmetry: once the primary and secondary structure is already pointing in one direction, these lines can make that direction more intelligible without being allowed to govern it.

### Why The Case Is Cumulative
[synthesis] The case is cumulative because the biblical material does not present the full profile in one place. Office, speech, timing, lineage, geography, expectation context, and corroborative fit are distributed across multiple passages and clusters. A cumulative argument here is not a way of hiding weakness. It is the correct argumentative form for a data set that is itself distributed and layered.

[synthesis] Convergence matters because the clusters are not all doing the same job. The primary clusters establish the direct prophetic profile. The braided Arabian secondary complex narrows the field of plausible candidates. The expectation clusters explain why the profile remains live rather than automatically closed by later interpretive traditions. The corroborative clusters add fit after the central structure is already standing. Because the functions differ, convergence increases pressure without automatically becoming double-counting.

[control] Weakening one line therefore does not collapse the whole case unless the weakened line is load-bearing. If [Baca And Pilgrimage](arguments/baca-and-pilgrimage/summary.md) fails, the direct profile remains. If lexical material in [Mahmad And Praised-One](arguments/mahmad-and-praised-one/summary.md) fails, the thesis remains intact. If one geography line weakens, other parts of the braided narrowing complex may still constrain the field. The case materially collapses only if the primary direct clusters are exhausted by their strongest rival readings, or if the secondary convergence dissolves into unrelated motifs that no longer point in the same direction.

[inference] This is why the repository's judgment is neither maximalist nor fragile. Each layer answers a different question: the primary clusters identify the kind of figure under discussion, the braided secondary complex narrows the field, the expectation clusters resist premature closure, and the corroborative lines deepen fit without governing the result. Openness alone is not the conclusion. The conclusion follows only when a still-open primary profile and a braided narrowing complex continue to point in the same historical direction. The repository therefore does not claim that every promoted passage independently names the Prophet ﷺ. It claims that once the material is ranked correctly, the strongest surviving lines converge most coherently on the Prophet ﷺ within the possibilities presently mapped in the repository.

### Failure Modes To Avoid
- Do not let lexical resemblance substitute for convergence.
- Do not cite a single place-name as if it proves the full case.
- Do not hide the Holy Spirit reading of the Paraclete.
- Do not treat Acts 3, Gospel Malachi uses, or John-the-Baptist readings as irrelevant.
- Do not promote broader Christology or law-control material as if it were direct prophecy.
- Do not double-count dependent synthesis as independent evidence.
- Do not let alternative readings dominate the presentation; they must discipline the case, not replace its forward argument.
- Do not quote or cite working corpus files in public-facing pages.

### Bottom Line
[inference] The repository's final judgment is not that the Bible yields a single effortless proof text naming the Prophet ﷺ, nor that the major Jewish and Christian alternatives are trivial. It is that, once the material is disciplined by rank, controls, and non-double-counting, the surviving primary profile is best described as a Moses-like bearer of received words and a post-Jesus guide or witness whose speech is not self-originated and whose mission remains tethered to witness to Jesus. [inference] If those primary closures are not exhaustive, the braided Arabian, Ishmaelite, and Kedar-facing narrowing complex explains why the cumulative case lands where it does. On the possibilities presently mapped in the repository, that combined profile is most coherently satisfied by the Prophet ﷺ, and is therefore academically serious, argumentatively coherent, and more than the sum of its individual passages.
