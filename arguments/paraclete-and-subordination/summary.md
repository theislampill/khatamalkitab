# Paraclete And Subordination

## Central Thesis
[control] This cluster tests whether Johannine dependence and mediated-speech language should shape the Paraclete reading. It does not identify the Prophet ﷺ directly; it prevents later theological shorthand from erasing the text's own agency and speech categories.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[John 12:49](../../bible/nt/john/ch12/v49/summary.md) and [John 16:13](../../bible/nt/john/ch16/v13/summary.md) foreground received speech. [John 14:28](../../bible/nt/john/ch14/v28/summary.md), [John 17:3](../../bible/nt/john/ch17/v3/summary.md), [John 20:17](../../bible/nt/john/ch20/v17/summary.md), and [Mark 13:32](../../bible/nt/mark/ch13/v32/summary.md) provide dependence/subordination pressure. [John 1:1](../../bible/nt/john/ch1/v1/summary.md) and [John 17:5](../../bible/nt/john/ch17/v5/summary.md) supply counter-pressure from high Christology and preexistence readings.

## Control Pressure
The cluster keeps the Paraclete argument from being dismissed by assuming all Johannine dependence language is irrelevant. At the same time, it blocks overreach: subordination texts are controls and interpretive context, not independent prophecy.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
Orthodox Christian interpretation integrates dependence language into Trinitarian mission, incarnation, and intra-divine relation. If that reading fully accounts for the data, these pages remain Christology controls and do not strengthen a later-prophet reading.

## Inference Boundary
[anchored] The dependence and high-Christology passages are real controls. [synthesis] They test interpretive assumptions around the Paraclete. They are not direct prophecy pages.

## Load-Bearing Status
Control/supportive and dependent on the Paraclete cluster. It is not an independent proof.

## Failure Modes
- Do not turn Christology controls into prophecy claims.
- Do not ignore John 1:1 or John 17:5.
- Do not use subordination language to avoid the specific Paraclete promises.
