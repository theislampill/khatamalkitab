# Messenger Before Arrival

## Central Thesis
[synthesis] Malachi's messenger, covenant, purification, Moses-memory, and Elijah-before-the-day material supplies a contested expectation frame. It supports the thesis only as expectation context because the Gospels strongly route this material through John the Baptist and Jesus.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[Malachi 3:1-4](../../bible/ot/malachi/ch3/v1-4/summary.md) joins messenger, sudden arrival, covenant, and purification. [Malachi 4:4-6](../../bible/ot/malachi/ch4/v4-6/summary.md) joins Moses-memory with Elijah-before-the-day expectation. [John 1:19-21](../../bible/nt/john/ch1/v19-21/summary.md) shows Messiah, Elijah, and “the Prophet” as distinguishable expectation categories. [Mark 1:2-3](../../bible/nt/mark/ch1/v2-3/summary.md), [Luke 7:26-28](../../bible/nt/luke/ch7/v26-28/summary.md), [Matthew 11:11-15](../../bible/nt/matthew/ch11/v11-15/summary.md), and [Matthew 17:10-13](../../bible/nt/matthew/ch17/v10-13/summary.md) supply the main Christian fulfillment control.

## Cumulative Profile
The cluster matters because it shows an expectation field involving messenger, covenant, restoration, Elijah, and Moses. It does not become direct proof because the Gospel material itself gives a powerful John-the-Baptist/Jesus synthesis.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
The strongest Christian reading says John the Baptist is the preparatory messenger/Elijah figure and Jesus is the one prepared for. Jewish/local readings may place Malachi in post-exilic temple, priestly, and covenant-purification contexts.

## Inference Boundary
[anchored] Malachi and the Gospel control passages are real expectation texts. [synthesis] Their value for the Prophet ﷺ is medium and dependent. [speculative] Later-event mappings such as temple-journey claims require separate review and are not anchored here.

## Load-Bearing Status
Expectation support and control. It should not carry the thesis independently.

## Failure Modes
- Do not hide the John-the-Baptist/Jesus reading.
- Do not turn temple language into later-event proof without separate support.
- Do not make Malachi stronger than Deuteronomy 18 or Paraclete.
