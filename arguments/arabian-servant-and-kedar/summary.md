# Arabian Servant and Kedar

## Cluster Claim
This cluster gathers the Isaiah 42 material that is read as pointing to a servant figure connected with Kedar, broader Gentile mission, law, anti-idolatry, and public triumph.

## Main Use
- Separate the full servant-song argument from single-verse location claims.
- Keep Kedar identification and anti-idolatry claims attached to the larger chapter.
- Provide a stable cluster for comparing Isaiah 42 with Paraclete material.

## Objections / Alternate Readings
- The servant is often read as Israel or as Jesus.
- The move from Kedar reference to Muhammad remains an interpretive step, not an automatic conclusion.
