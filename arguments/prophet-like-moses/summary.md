# Prophet Like Moses

## Central Thesis
[anchored] Deuteronomy 18:15-22 is the repository's primary Torah-side prophecy cluster. [synthesis] It creates pressure by joining office, speech, and accountability: God raises the prophet, the prophet is like Moses, the prophet speaks received divine words, and the hearer is answerable for refusing that speech.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[Deuteronomy 18:15-22](../../bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v15-22/summary.md) supplies the promise and the prophetic-test frame. [Deuteronomy 34:10](../../bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch34/v10/summary.md) keeps the Moses-like category open or contested inside the Torah horizon. [John 1:19-21](../../bible/nt/john/ch1/v19-21/summary.md) shows that “the Prophet” could be treated as a recognizable expectation category, and [Acts 3:22-23](../../bible/nt/acts/ch3/v22-23/summary.md) preserves the major Christian application to Jesus.

## Cumulative Profile
The cluster does not merely say “a prophet will come.” Its force is that a candidate must satisfy several constraints at once: Moses-like public mission, received revelation, law-bearing authority, covenantal accountability, and a plausible reading of “from their brothers.” The Ishmaelite support pages do not prove the claim, but they matter because they test whether that final constraint can extend beyond an Israel-only reading.

## Convergence Test
- Deuteronomy 18:15-19 gives the positive promise: raised prophet, likeness to Moses, divine words in the mouth, and required obedience.
- Deuteronomy 18:20-22 gives the negative control: presumption and failed speech test prophecy claims rather than identifying the candidate.
- Deuteronomy 34:10 prevents a quick claim that the Moses-like category was already exhausted.
- John 1:19-21 keeps “the Prophet” distinct from Messiah and Elijah in an expectation scene.
- Acts 3:22-23 confirms Deuteronomy 18 remained live, while also forcing engagement with the Jesus-centered reading.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
The strongest rival synthesis says Deuteronomy 18 addresses Israel's need for prophets instead of divination, that the prophet must arise from within Israel's covenant community, and that Acts 3 authoritatively identifies the passage with Jesus. This rival reading is not a footnote; it is a control on the whole cluster.

## Inference Boundary
[anchored] The Deuteronomy promise is direct evidence. [synthesis] Identifying the Prophet ﷺ depends on the whole cluster and its brethren, expectation, and control links. [inference] Calling this one of the strongest clusters is a repository-level judgment, not a single-verse fact.

## Load-Bearing Status
Primary and load-bearing. If this cluster fails entirely, the Torah-side spine of the repository is materially weakened.

## Failure Modes
- Do not make “brethren” carry the argument without answering the Israelite-only reading.
- Do not erase Acts 3 as a serious Christian counter-reading.
- Do not treat Deuteronomy 18:20-22 as direct identification; it is a criterion and control unit.
