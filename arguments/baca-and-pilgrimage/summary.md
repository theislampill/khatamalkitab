# Baca And Pilgrimage

`Status:` [inference]. `Rank:` corroborative and tentative.

## Cluster Claim

This cluster preserves the repo's sacred-geography resonance line. It is not meant to function as a direct proof or as a decisive geography argument. Its proper use is later and cumulative: once the primary profile and the main narrowing complex are already in place, pilgrimage and sanctuary imagery may add a secondary sense of fit around sacred location and gathered devotion.

## Why This Cluster Still Has Value

[Psalms 84:4-10](../../bible/ot/psalms/ch84/v4-10/summary.md) and [Psalms 84:6](../../bible/ot/psalms/ch84/v6/summary.md) provide the core Baca and pilgrimage language. [Isaiah 60:1-12](../../bible/ot/isaiah/ch60/v1-12/summary.md) and [Song of Songs 6:2](../../bible/ot/song-of-songs/ch6/v2/summary.md) matter only as secondary atmosphere around gathering, sanctuary, or sacred-location imagery. The cluster should therefore remain deliberately narrow and carefully ranked.

Even at that rank, the cluster is not empty. Its value lies in the way it can deepen the feel of the cumulative case once an Arabian field has already been made plausible elsewhere. The line contributes not by proving a destination but by adding sacred-geography resonance after stronger directional work has already been done. That distinction matters. The cluster is useful precisely when it is not being asked to do more than it can carry.
