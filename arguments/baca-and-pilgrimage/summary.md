# Baca And Pilgrimage

## Central Thesis
[inference] This is sacred-geography resonance, not direct proof. Psalm 84's Baca and pilgrimage-to-house-of-God imagery can matter only after the Arabian geography cluster is already established.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[Psalms 84:4-10](../../bible/ot/psalms/ch84/v4-10/summary.md) supplies pilgrimage longing, blessed travel, Baca, and house-of-God language. [Psalms 84:6](../../bible/ot/psalms/ch84/v6/summary.md) is the atomic Baca node. [Isaiah 60:1-12](../../bible/ot/isaiah/ch60/v1-12/summary.md) and [Song of Songs 6:2](../../bible/ot/song-of-songs/ch6/v2/summary.md) are included only as secondary atmosphere around sacred location, gathering, and garden/pasture imagery.

## Actual Pressure
The convergence does not prove a location; it asks whether pilgrimage longing, a valley named Baca, and house-of-God devotion can coherently resonate with an Arabian sanctuary once Paran/Arabia has already narrowed the field. It fails as support if Baca is figurative, local, unknown, or Zion-centered, because then the cluster becomes devotional atmosphere rather than geographic corroboration.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
Baca may be figurative, local, unknown, or Zion-centered. Pilgrimage imagery may belong entirely to Israel's temple worship and not to Makkah.

## Inference Boundary
[anchored] Psalm 84 contains Baca/pilgrimage language. [inference] Makkah-oriented use is tentative and dependent.

## Load-Bearing Status
Corroborative, dependent, and non-load-bearing.

## Failure Modes
- Do not treat Baca as self-evidently Makkah.
- Do not let pilgrimage imagery replace direct prophecy arguments.
- Do not make Isaiah 60 or Song of Songs carry geographic proof.
