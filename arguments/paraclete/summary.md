# Paraclete

## Central Thesis
[anchored] John 14-16 contains the repository's primary Gospel-side promise cluster. [synthesis] It creates pressure by tying the promised Paraclete to Jesus' departure and to a recognizable mission-pattern: guiding, witnessing, teaching/reminding, disclosing future things, and speaking what is received rather than self-originated.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[John 14:16-17](../../bible/nt/john/ch14/v16-17/summary.md) introduces “another” helper and the Spirit-of-truth frame. [John 14:26](../../bible/nt/john/ch14/v26/summary.md) adds teaching and remembrance. [John 15:26](../../bible/nt/john/ch15/v26/summary.md) adds witness. [John 16:7-13](../../bible/nt/john/ch16/v7-13/summary.md) adds departure, guidance into truth, future disclosure, and non-autonomous speech. The cluster is tested against explicit Spirit/Pentecost controls rather than isolated from them.

## Cumulative Profile
The argument is not just “Paraclete means praised one.” The load-bearing profile is functional: later arrival after Jesus, guidance into truth, witness to Jesus, and received speech. [Deuteronomy 18:18](../../bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v18/summary.md) matters because it turns “received speech” from a Johannine feature into a cross-canon comparison point, while the Spirit/Pentecost controls test whether that comparison is necessary or overextended.

## Convergence Test
- John 14 contributes another-helper and Spirit-of-truth language.
- John 14:26 contributes teaching and remembrance of Jesus' words.
- John 15:26 contributes witness from the Father concerning Jesus.
- John 16:7-13 contributes departure, guidance, future disclosure, and received speech.
- Deuteronomy 18 contributes a matching received-speech prophet profile, but only as synthesis.
- Spirit/Pentecost pages test whether John itself already supplies the fulfillment.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
The strongest Christian reading identifies the Paraclete with the Holy Spirit promised to the disciples, abiding with or in them, teaching and reminding them of Jesus' words, and realized in post-resurrection Spirit reception and Pentecost/community witness. This alternative remains strong enough that the cluster is primary but objection-sensitive.

## Inference Boundary
[anchored] John 14-16 is a real promise cluster. [synthesis] Identification with the Prophet ﷺ requires cross-linking with Deuteronomy 18 and must survive the internal Johannine Spirit reading. [speculative] Lexical replacement arguments are non-load-bearing unless independently reviewed.

## Load-Bearing Status
Primary and load-bearing, but objection-sensitive. If the Spirit/Pentecost reading fully exhausts the Paraclete promises, this cluster loses its direct force.

## Failure Modes
- Do not reduce the argument to Parakletos/Periklytos wordplay.
- Do not ignore John 7:39, John 20:22, Luke 24:49, or 1 John 4:1-6.
- Do not call surrounding Johannine control verses direct prophecy.
