# Paraclete

## Central Thesis
[anchored] John 14-16 contains the repository's primary Gospel-side promise cluster. [synthesis] It creates pressure by tying the promised Paraclete to Jesus' departure and to a recognizable mission-pattern: coming after Jesus' departure, exposing or convicting the world, guiding, witnessing, disclosing future things, and speaking what is received rather than self-originated.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[John 14:16-17](../../bible/nt/john/ch14/v16-17/summary.md) introduces “another” helper and the Spirit-of-truth frame. [John 14:26](../../bible/nt/john/ch14/v26/summary.md) adds teaching and remembrance. [John 15:26](../../bible/nt/john/ch15/v26/summary.md) adds witness. [John 16:7-13](../../bible/nt/john/ch16/v7-13/summary.md) adds departure, world-conviction, deferred disclosure, future disclosure, and non-autonomous speech. [John 12:49](../../bible/nt/john/ch12/v49/summary.md) matters as an internal Johannine control because Jesus himself is described as speaking received rather than self-originated words. The cluster is tested against explicit Spirit/Pentecost controls rather than isolated from them.

## Cumulative Profile
The argument is not just “Paraclete means praised one.” The load-bearing profile is sequential and functional: departure-conditioned coming, world-facing conviction, guidance into truth, witness to Jesus, future disclosure, and received speech. That witness-to-Jesus feature is candidate-filtering pressure, not mere color: any later figure who negates Jesus rather than bears witness to him is excluded by the cluster's own terms. [John 12:49](../../bible/nt/john/ch12/v49/summary.md) matters because it makes non-self-originated speech an internal Johannine feature before any cross-canon comparison is made. [Deuteronomy 18:18](../../bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v18/summary.md) then matters because it turns that speech-profile into a cross-canon comparison point, while the Spirit/Pentecost controls test whether the comparison is necessary or overextended. The real question is whether Pentecost and Johannine Spirit language exhaust that whole sequence or leave a remainder that still needs explanation.

## Convergence Test
- John 14 contributes another-helper and Spirit-of-truth language.
- John 14:26 contributes teaching and remembrance of Jesus' words.
- John 15:26 contributes witness from the Father concerning Jesus.
- John 16:8-13 contributes public conviction of the world, deferred disclosure, future disclosure, and received speech after Jesus' departure.
- John 12:49 shows that received speech already belongs to Johannine revelation language.
- Deuteronomy 18 contributes a matching received-speech prophet profile, but only as synthesis.
- Acts 2:1-4, John 7:39, John 20:22, Luke 24:49, and 1 John 4:1-6 test whether John itself already supplies the fulfillment, while allowing for the possibility that Pentecost is real without exhausting every feature in dispute.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
The strongest Christian reading identifies the Paraclete with the Holy Spirit promised to the disciples, abiding with or in them, teaching and reminding them of Jesus' words, convicting through apostolic witness, and realized in post-resurrection Spirit reception and Pentecost/community witness. This alternative remains strong enough that the cluster is primary but objection-sensitive, and the cluster survives only if that internal Johannine reading is not exhaustive.

## Inference Boundary
[anchored] John 14-16 is a real promise cluster. [synthesis] Identification with the Prophet ﷺ requires cross-linking with Deuteronomy 18 and must survive the internal Johannine Spirit reading. [speculative] Lexical replacement arguments are non-load-bearing unless independently reviewed.

## Load-Bearing Status
Primary and load-bearing, but objection-sensitive. If the Spirit/Pentecost reading fully exhausts the Paraclete promises, this cluster loses its direct force.

## Failure Modes
- Do not reduce the argument to Parakletos/Periklytos wordplay.
- Do not ignore Acts 2:1-4, John 7:39, John 12:49, John 20:22, Luke 24:49, or 1 John 4:1-6.
- Do not call surrounding Johannine control verses direct prophecy.
