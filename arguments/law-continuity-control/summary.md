# Law Continuity Control

## Central Thesis
[control] This cluster is a methodological guardrail. It asks whether later-fulfillment claims preserve continuity with prior revelation, covenant, and law categories rather than relying on simple replacement language.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[Matthew 5:17-19](../../bible/nt/matthew/ch5/v17-19/summary.md) raises the issue of Torah and prophetic fulfillment. [Acts 21:20-26](../../bible/nt/acts/ch21/v20-26/summary.md) shows early Jesus-followers negotiating Torah observance and community perception. [Malachi 3:3](../../bible/ot/malachi/ch3/v3/summary.md) adds priestly purification context.

## Control Pressure
The cluster does not prove the Prophet ﷺ. It prevents claims about fulfillment from becoming crude displacement claims. Any cumulative case must be able to say how later fulfillment relates to earlier revelation, law, and covenant discipline.

## Anchored Passages
- [Matthew 5:17-19](../../bible/nt/matthew/ch5/v17-19/summary.md)
- [Acts 21:20-26](../../bible/nt/acts/ch21/v20-26/summary.md)
- [Malachi 3:3](../../bible/ot/malachi/ch3/v3/summary.md)

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
Matthew 5 may be read as Jesus fulfilling Torah within Christian theology. Acts 21 may be pastoral accommodation rather than binding law-continuity. Malachi 3 may remain post-exilic priestly purification.

## Inference Boundary
[control] These passages discipline the argument. They are not direct prophecy evidence and should not be promoted as such.

## Load-Bearing Status
Control-only, but methodologically important. It is load-bearing as a constraint, not as evidence.

## Failure Modes
- Do not promote law-control material as direct prophecy.
- Do not use these controls to evade direct passage interpretation.
- Do not erase Christian and Jewish legal-theological readings.
