# Law Continuity Control

`Status:` [control]. `Rank:` control-only but architecturally important.

## Control Claim
This cluster keeps the repo's fulfillment language disciplined. Its role is not to identify the Prophet (peace be upon him). Its role is to ensure that the larger case is not argued through crude replacement rhetoric that ignores law, covenant, purification, or continuity categories.

## Why This Control Matters
[Matthew 5:17-19](../../bible/nt/matthew/ch5/v17-19/summary.md) raises the relation between fulfillment and enduring law. [Acts 21:20-26](../../bible/nt/acts/ch21/v20-26/summary.md) shows early Jesus-followers negotiating Torah observance and communal reputation. [Malachi 3:3](../../bible/ot/malachi/ch3/v3/summary.md) adds purification and covenantal discipline. Read together, these passages make one important methodological point: a later claim of fulfillment must still explain how it relates to earlier revelation rather than acting as though continuity questions never arise.

## Constructive Use
Because this cluster is a control, its contribution is indirect but real. It prevents the repo from weakening its own case through careless framing. The cumulative argument is stronger when it shows how later guidance, public order, and covenantal seriousness can be discussed without sounding like mere law-erasure or rhetorical displacement.

## Networked Significance
This cluster matters most inside [Anti-Closure Convergence](../../convergences/anti-closure-convergence/summary.md), where it works with [Live Prophetic Expectation](../live-prophetic-expectation/summary.md) and [Messenger Before Arrival](../messenger-before-arrival/summary.md). It also stabilizes [Public Guidance Convergence](../../convergences/public-guidance-convergence/summary.md) by reminding the repo that broad guidance claims must remain tethered to continuity rather than abstraction.

## Objection Note
The main rival readings remain live: Matthew 5 can be read within Christian fulfillment theology, Acts 21 within pastoral accommodation, and Malachi 3 within post-exilic purification. That is precisely why this cluster should remain a control cluster. Its point is not to overturn those readings but to keep the repo from presenting its constructive case in a careless or overly simplified way.

## Contribution Summary
This cluster strengthens the thesis by protecting its mode of presentation. It keeps the repo from reaching for easy slogans and helps the larger cumulative case sound more coherent, serious, and covenant-aware.
