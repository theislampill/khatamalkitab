# Kingdom Transfer

## Central Thesis
[synthesis] This cluster is a broad receptivity frame, not a tight proof. It asks whether several texts permit a movement of rule, fruit-bearing, or covenantal leadership to another people or kingdom, while admitting that the passages do not all describe the same kind of transfer.

## Why These Passages Belong Together
[Genesis 49:10](../../bible/ot/genesis/ch49/v10/summary.md) contributes rulership expectation. [Daniel 2:44](../../bible/ot/daniel/ch2/v44/summary.md) and [Daniel 7:17-25](../../bible/ot/daniel/ch7/v17-25/summary.md) contribute kingdom succession and empire-cycle language. [Matthew 21:42-44](../../bible/nt/matthew/ch21/v42-44/summary.md) contributes rejected-stone and kingdom-transfer language. [Isaiah 60:1-12](../../bible/ot/isaiah/ch60/v1-12/summary.md), [Zechariah 9:9-10](../../bible/ot/zechariah/ch9/v9-10/summary.md), and [Haggai 2:6-9](../../bible/ot/haggai/ch2/v6-9/summary.md) widen the frame through Zion/restoration, rule, peace, and sanctuary motifs.

## Actual Pressure
The convergence here does not identify the Prophet ﷺ; it changes the background plausibility of transfer. Genesis 49 keeps rulership expectation open, Daniel shows kingdoms yielding to a final divine kingdom, and Matthew 21 explicitly frames rejected stewardship and fruit-bearing as transferable. Read together, they create room for covenantal authority moving to another people, but only as a broad frame that depends on the direct prophecy clusters for identification.

## Strongest Alternative Synthesis
Genesis 49 may be Judah/Davidic messiah expectation. Daniel may be Second Temple empire schema. Matthew 21 is commonly read as Jesus, church, or Gentile mission. Isaiah 60, Zechariah 9, and Haggai may remain Zion/restoration, messianic peace, or Second Temple glory texts.

## Inference Boundary
[synthesis] The cluster shows transfer and culmination motifs. [inference] Applying those motifs to Islam or the Prophet ﷺ is secondary and broad.

## Load-Bearing Status
Medium supportive motif, dependent and non-decisive.

## Failure Modes
- Do not merge unlike transfer texts into one proof.
- Do not ignore anchor-specific alternatives.
- Do not inflate political fulfillment beyond the text.
