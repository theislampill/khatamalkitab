# Prophet Like Moses and Paraclete

## Cluster Claim
This higher-order page tracks the recurring move in the working corpus that links Deuteronomy 18 and John 14-16 into one continuity argument: the prophet expected after Moses is revisited in Jesus' farewell discourse.

## Depends On
- `arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md`
- `arguments/paraclete/summary.md`
- `bible/ot/deuteronomy/ch18/v18-19/summary.md`
- `bible/nt/john/ch16/v7-13/summary.md`

## Main Use
- Keep composite reasoning out of a single verse folder.
- Show how "put my words in his mouth" and "he will not speak on his own" are paired in the working corpus.
- Record the claim that the New Testament reopens, rather than closes, the Deuteronomy 18 expectation.

## Objections / Alternate Readings
- This is a strong synthesis claim, not a narrow passage claim.
- It inherits all major objections from both the Deuteronomy 18 and Paraclete clusters.
