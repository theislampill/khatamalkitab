# Candidate-Field Narrowing

`Status:` [synthesis]. `Rank:` high-value convergence.

## Convergence Claim
This convergence gathers the three main secondary narrowing clusters: servant law-to-nations pressure, lineage eligibility, and Arabian geography. Its importance lies in the fact that these clusters do not do the same job. Together they narrow the candidate field by mission shape, kinship eligibility, and regional direction.

## What Each Side Adds
[Arabian Servant And Kedar](../../arguments/arabian-servant-and-kedar/summary.md) adds a public mission profile joined to Kedar-facing material. [Ishmaelite Line And Brethren](../../arguments/ishmaelite-line-and-brethren/summary.md) adds candidate eligibility and kinship pressure. [Paran And Arabia](../../arguments/paran-and-arabia/summary.md) adds geographic direction through repeated Arabian and Paran-related markers. This is what gives the convergence its force: each component narrows the field differently.

## Why This Is Not Redundancy
This convergence would be weak if all three clusters simply repeated "Arabia." They do not. One asks what sort of mission is in view. One asks which field remains eligible. One asks where the surviving field points. Because the functions differ, the cumulative narrowing is stronger than any one secondary cluster read alone.

## Objection Note
The main objection is also structural: geography can be overstated, lineage can be mistaken for proof, and servant/Kedar language can be detached from its local alternatives. That objection is handled here by the convergence itself. The point is not that any one of these clusters settles the matter. The point is that together they create a historically directional field once the primary profile remains live.

## Contribution Summary
This convergence strengthens the thesis by giving the repo a disciplined route from direct profile to plausible candidate field. It is the main reason the cumulative argument looks historically directional rather than abstract.
