# Primary Profile Convergence

`Status:` [synthesis]. `Rank:` high-value convergence.

## Convergence Claim
This convergence brings together the two primary direct profiles in the repository: the Mosaic office profile and the Johannine later-guide profile. Its claim is not that the two clusters say exactly the same thing. Its claim is that, once each cluster survives its own main controls, they converge on a coherent question about a later bearer of received guidance, public authority, and accountable speech.

## What Each Side Adds
[Prophet Like Moses](../../arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md) contributes office, Moses-likeness, received speech, and covenantal accountability. [Paraclete](../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) contributes post-Jesus timing, witness to Jesus, future disclosure, and non-autonomous speech. The convergence matters because the second cluster does not merely repeat the first. It adds a later-Gospel profile that the first cluster cannot provide on its own.

## Why This Is Not Redundancy
If the two clusters merely repeated "a prophet will come," there would be no real convergence. What makes this a true convergence is the addition of distinct constraints. The Torah-side line defines the kind of office being sought. The Gospel-side line adds the timing, mission-shape, and witness logic of a later guide. Read together, they create a more constrained cumulative profile than either cluster could create alone.

## Objection Note
The main objection belongs here in full form: the two clusters may remain only analogous, or they may each be absorbed by their strongest internal closure readings. That objection is real, but it is exactly why convergence pages matter. This page tests whether the surviving remainder of each cluster still points in the same direction once closure pressure has already been faced at cluster level.

## Contribution Summary
This convergence strengthens the thesis by turning two major profile lines into one coherent cumulative question. It gives the repo a genuine direct-profile convergence before the argument moves into narrowing, anti-closure support, and final synthesis.
