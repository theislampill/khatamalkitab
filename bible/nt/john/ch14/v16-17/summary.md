# John 14:16-17

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` opening Paraclete range with direct promise and major comparison pressure. `Contribution:` direct anchor with strong Spirit-reading pressure.

## Claim
This range opens the Paraclete material by introducing another helper and the truth-bearing frame in one local promise. It matters because it sets both the positive profile and the main internal comparison that later Paraclete pages must address. In that sense, it is both an anchor and an interpretive hinge.

## Reasoning
Read together, the verses do more than mention help in general. They introduce another figure in relation to Jesus, place that figure in a truth-bearing role, and immediately raise the question of how the promise relates to the disciples and to the Spirit-language of the passage. That is why the range belongs at the front of the Paraclete sequence and why later pages build on it rather than bypassing it.

## Local Significance
At local level, John 14:16-17 establishes the opening promise-structure the later discourse will develop. It gives the reader the first concentrated encounter with "another" and the truth-bearing frame, which is why the repo treats the range as more important than either verse read as an isolated fragment.

## Networked Significance
Within the wider repository, this range anchors [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) and contributes to [Primary Profile Convergence](../../../../../convergences/primary-profile-convergence/summary.md) by preserving the opening Gospel-side promise structure before the fuller later-guide profile unfolds in John 16.

## Objection Note
The main competing reading is the Holy Spirit abiding with or in the disciples. That objection is unavoidable and belongs here because it governs the whole Paraclete discussion from the start. Even so, the range still contributes a major opening frame for the Gospel-side argument, because the later question is whether that opening frame is fully exhausted by the standard closure reading.

## Contribution Summary
Used this way, John 14:16-17 strengthens the thesis by securing the opening promise structure of the Paraclete line and by preparing the Gospel-side argument for its fuller development in John 16.
