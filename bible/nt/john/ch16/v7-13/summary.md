# John 16:7-13

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` primary Paraclete range; direct profile with internal control pressure. `Contribution:` direct anchor with embedded Spirit and Pentecost comparison.

## Claim

This passage is the densest Gospel-side statement of the later-guide profile. It gathers Jesus' departure, the coming of the promised figure, public conviction or exposure, guidance into truth, future disclosure, and speech that is received rather than self-originated. Read as a range, it becomes the most important local unit in the Paraclete material.

## Reasoning

The range has force because its elements form one progression instead of a loose list. Jesus' departure creates the timing frame. That matters because the later figure is not simply a restatement of what is already present. `vv.8-11` then add a world-facing function rather than a merely inward one. Whatever the exact nuance of conviction or exposure, the work reaches beyond a private circle. `vv.12-13` add deferred disclosure, guidance into truth, and speech from what is heard. Those features make the range unusually concentrated. They join later arrival, public effect, fuller guidance, future-oriented speech, and delegated utterance in one passage.

Taken together, these elements explain why the repository treats John 16:7-13 as a major direct anchor rather than as an incidental support text. The point is not that one phrase is decisive by itself. The point is that the whole later-guide sequence is unusually full here.

## Local Significance

At local level, John 16:7-13 does more than repeat the earlier Paraclete sayings. It concentrates the features that make the range uniquely important: later arrival, world-facing work, fuller guidance, future disclosure, and received speech. That is what makes it the strongest Gospel-side bridge to the broader later-guide case in the repo.

## Boundary

The main competing reading is that this range describes the Holy Spirit's post-resurrection and Pentecost work among the disciples and early community. That reading is the most serious internal rival and belongs here. Even so, the passage still contributes a major constructive profile because the dispute turns on whether the whole later-guide sequence is exhausted by that reading, not on whether the range has any direct force at all.

## Contribution Summary

John 16:7-13 strengthens the thesis by securing the repository's strongest Gospel-side later-guide profile. It gives the cumulative case a second direct anchor before the argument moves into convergence and candidate-field narrowing.
