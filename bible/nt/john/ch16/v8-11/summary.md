# John 16:8-11

## Page Type
Local combined passage page

## Passage-Specific Contribution
- This range contributes the conviction triad: the world is convicted concerning sin, righteousness, and judgment.
- It matters because the Paraclete sequence is publicly forensic and adversarial, not only inwardly consoling or mnemonic.
- The strongest alternative is that Jesus is describing the Holy Spirit's convicting work through the disciples and early church.

## Why This Range Matters
- The range matters because `v.8` announces conviction and `vv.9-11` specify the three predicates that define it. Split apart, the triad loses its local argumentative shape.

## Epistemic Classification
- Status: anchored
- Passage contribution: direct sub-unit within the primary Paraclete range
- Role: conviction triad / world-facing pressure

## Argumentative Use
- Use this range to show that the Paraclete promise includes public exposure of the world in relation to Jesus.
- The range feeds [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) by sharpening the cluster's world-facing dimension and candidate-filtering pressure.

- The strongest local alternative is that the conviction triad is fulfilled by the Holy Spirit's post-resurrection work through the disciples and apostolic proclamation.
