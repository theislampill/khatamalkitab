# John 1:19-21

## Claim Use
This local range is used to argue that, at the time of John the Baptist, "the Christ," Elijah, and "the Prophet" could still be heard as distinguishable expectations.

## Why This Range Matters Together
- The force of the argument depends on the three-way distinction inside the exchange.
- The range is repeatedly used as a New Testament witness back to Deuteronomy 18.

## Objections / Alternate Readings
- Some readers treat the distinctions as temporary or stylistic rather than as proof of permanently separate roles.
- The passage does not identify the prophet by name; it only preserves the expectation.
