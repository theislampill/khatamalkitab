# John 20:22

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` post-resurrection Spirit-gift control. `Contribution:` direct Johannine closure anchor.

## Claim
This verse matters because the risen Jesus explicitly gives the Holy Spirit to his disciples. It is one of the strongest internal controls on any claim that the Paraclete promises remain entirely unfulfilled within the Johannine narrative.

## Reasoning
The scene is direct and concrete. Jesus breathes on the disciples and tells them to receive the Holy Spirit. That means the Gospel itself narrates a bestowal event rather than leaving all Spirit language hanging in the future. This does not settle every Johannine predicate by itself, but it does set a real boundary. Any later-guide reading has to explain why this event is not simply ignored.

## Local Significance
Locally, John 20:22 is important because it makes the closure reading textual and immediate. The rival reading is not a distant theological abstraction. It is written into the narrative itself.

## Networked Significance
This page strengthens [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) by forcing that cluster to reckon with a concrete post-resurrection Spirit gift. It therefore sharpens the Gospel-side argument instead of weakening it through vagueness.


## Contribution Summary
John 20:22 strengthens the thesis indirectly by making the main Johannine rival more exact. The larger case becomes more persuasive when this verse is treated as a real control rather than as a disposable inconvenience.
