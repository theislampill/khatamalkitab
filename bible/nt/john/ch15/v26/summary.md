# John 15:26

## Claim Use
This verse is used as a middle Paraclete anchor: the Spirit of truth proceeds from the Father and testifies regarding Jesus.

## Why This Passage Gets Its Own Node
- It is often cited alongside John 14:26 and John 16:13.
- It sharpens the "testimony about Jesus" function without carrying all of John 16's later mission language.

## Objections / Alternate Readings
- Many readers still fold the verse directly into a Holy-Spirit reading.
- By itself it does not answer the departure condition or the later "hears and speaks" language from John 16.
