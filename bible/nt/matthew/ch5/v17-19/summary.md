# Matthew 5:17-19

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` law-continuity control. `Contribution:` control; keeps later fulfillment claims textually serious.

## Claim

This passage matters as a continuity control. It keeps the repo from speaking about later fulfillment as though earlier law and prophetic revelation could simply be discarded. Its value lies in forcing later claims to answer continuity questions rather than evade them.

## Reasoning

The range has force because fulfillment and continuity appear together and are not allowed to drift apart. Jesus speaks of fulfillment, then rejects abolition, then intensifies the seriousness of what already stands, and finally ties that seriousness to teaching and practice. The point is therefore stronger than a generic statement that scripture matters. The point is that later fulfillment language must explain how it relates to prior revelation instead of acting as though continuity questions are irrelevant. That makes this a control page rather than a direct-proof page.

## Local Significance

Read as a passage, Matthew 5:17-19 keeps together completion, continuity, and seriousness about what already stands. That local combination is why the page is more useful than a one-line slogan about law. It preserves the full tension the larger argument has to respect.
