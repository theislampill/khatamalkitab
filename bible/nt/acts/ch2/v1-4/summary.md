# Acts 2:1-4

## Page Type
Local combined passage page

## Passage-Specific Contribution
- This range contributes the core Pentecost event sequence: gathered disciples, wind-like manifestation, tongues-as-of-fire, Spirit-filling, and speech as the Spirit gives utterance.
- It matters as the clearest event-level control on the claim that John 14-16 was fulfilled by the Holy Spirit in the disciples and early community.
- The strongest alternative is that Pentecost is a real Spirit fulfillment event without necessarily exhausting every feature of the Johannine Paraclete sequence.

## Why This Range Matters
- The range matters because the Pentecost control is not one detail but one event sequence.

## Epistemic Classification
- Status: anchored
- Passage contribution: control; primary Pentecost fulfillment test
- Role: Paraclete control anchor / Pentecost event

## Argumentative Use
- Use this range to route the strongest Paraclete rival through an actual promoted event page rather than mentioning Pentecost only in cluster prose.
- The range feeds [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) by testing whether John 14-16 is exhausted by early Christian Spirit empowerment.
- Identification of the Prophet ﷺ from this passage remains cluster-level synthesis and must survive this control.

## Strongest Local Alternative
- The strongest local alternative is that Pentecost substantially fulfills the Paraclete promises in the disciples and early church. If that reading absorbs the whole Johannine sequence, the human-messenger reading loses direct force. If it does not absorb the whole sequence, the remainder still has to be explained.
