# Acts 2:4

`Status:` anchored.

## Claim
This verse states the heart of the Pentecost event: the disciples are filled with the Holy Spirit and begin to speak as the Spirit gives utterance. It matters because this is the clearest verse-level expression of the main closure reading for the Paraclete passages.

## Reasoning
The verse joins Spirit-filling to empowered speech in a definite historical moment. That is exactly why it is a serious control rather than a disposable objection. At the same time, it names one realized form of Spirit activity, not every Johannine predicate at once. The larger case therefore has to take this verse seriously without pretending it closes every question by itself.
