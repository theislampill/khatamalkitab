# Acts 2:1

`Status:` anchored.

## Claim
This verse matters because the Pentecost event begins in a gathered, appointed setting rather than in an isolated private experience. It opens the strongest event-level rival to later-guide readings of the Johannine promises.

## Reasoning
The communal setting is not incidental. It anchors the rival reading in a concrete historical moment with waiting disciples, shared witnesses, and public timing. That is why the verse works as a real control instead of a vague Pentecost reference.

## Networked Significance
This chiefly strengthens [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) and, secondarily, [Paraclete And Subordination](../../../../../arguments/paraclete-and-subordination/summary.md) by making the Pentecost control historically specific from the first verse.
