# Acts 21:24

`Status:` anchored.

## Claim
This verse explains the public action itself: Paul is to join the men, purify himself with them, and cover their expenses so the rumors can be seen as false. It matters because continuity is defended here by public observance.

## Reasoning
The verse gives the rebuttal concrete form. Paul is not merely said to respect continuity; he is asked to enact that respect visibly. This blocks overdrawn discontinuity readings and keeps the larger case historically disciplined.

## Networked Significance
This strongly supports [Law Continuity Control](../../../../../arguments/law-continuity-control/summary.md) because it makes the passage's continuity pressure practical rather than rhetorical.
