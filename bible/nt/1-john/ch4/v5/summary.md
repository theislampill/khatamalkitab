# 1 John 4:5

`Status:` anchored.

## Claim
This verse explains one mark of false speech: it speaks from the world, and the world listens to it. That matters because reception by itself is not a safe measure of truth.

## Reasoning
John distinguishes truthful witness from worldly resonance. A message can sound plausible, attractive, or successful and still be false. The verse therefore blocks any easy appeal to popularity as validation of spiritual claims.

## Networked Significance
This strengthens [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) by keeping the Gospel-side case tied to truth and witness rather than to the world's approval.
