# 1 John 4:4

`Status:` anchored.

## Claim
This verse adds confidence to the discernment sequence: the faithful are "of God," and the power with them is greater than the one in the world. It matters because truth is not measured only by visible pressure or numerical success.

## Reasoning
The verse does not cancel the need for testing. It explains why testing does not end in defeat. Error is real, but it is not ultimate. That gives the control layer a positive side: God can sustain true witness against rival voices.

## Networked Significance
This supports [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) by showing that Johannine discernment includes both scrutiny and confidence rather than mere suspicion.
