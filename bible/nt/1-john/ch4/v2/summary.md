# 1 John 4:2

`Status:` anchored.

## Claim
This verse provides the first positive discernment criterion: the true spirit confesses Jesus Christ as having come in the flesh. That matters because spiritual truth is tied to faithful witness about Jesus, not to intensity alone.

## Reasoning
John does not let the Spirit become an independent authority with its own detached message. The test binds true inspiration to a right confession about Jesus. That keeps later guidance subordinate to the witness it is supposed to confirm.

## Networked Significance
This strengthens [Paraclete](../../../../../arguments/paraclete/summary.md) and especially [Paraclete And Subordination](../../../../../arguments/paraclete-and-subordination/summary.md), because the Johannine line cannot be read as severing Spirit from Christ-centered testimony.
