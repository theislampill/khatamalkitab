# Malachi 3:1-4

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` expectation support and control-sensitive passage. `Contribution:` support; supplies sequence pressure and covenantal seriousness.

## Claim

This passage contributes messenger, sudden arrival, covenant, and purification language. It matters as expectation context because it holds preparation and arrival inside one sequence, though it remains constrained by Gospel John-the-Baptist and Jesus readings.

## Reasoning

Messenger, covenant, and purification are not scattered here as unrelated themes. They form one movement in which arrival is preceded by preparation and followed by refining consequence. That is why the passage matters for the wider repo. It does not simply add one more proof text to a pile. It gives the messenger-before-arrival cluster its clearest sequence logic and gives the law-continuity control a passage where covenantal seriousness and purification stay visible.

## Local Significance

Read as a passage, this range has more force than its atom pages read separately because the local force lies in the whole sequence. Messenger, arrival, covenant, and purification belong to the same scene. That is stronger than quoting one verse about a messenger in isolation, because it explains what kind of preparation the text actually envisions.
