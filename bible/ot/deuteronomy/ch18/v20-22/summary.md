# Deuteronomy 18:20-22

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` prophetic-test control within the Deuteronomy 18 promise. `Contribution:` office-accountability and verification boundary for the Mosaic line.

## Claim
This passage matters because the same Deuteronomy unit that promises a prophet like Moses also sets public tests for presumptuous speech and failed prediction. The office being sought is therefore accountable and verifiable. It cannot be reduced to generic inspiration or pious admiration.

## Reasoning
The promise and the test belong to the same legal-prophetic movement. After speaking of words placed in the prophet's mouth and of the need to hear him, the passage turns immediately to unauthorized speech and to the case where a claimed word does not come to pass. That sequence matters because it binds profile and criterion together. A Moses-like office is not only exalted. It is exposed to public accountability.

## Local Significance
Locally, this page makes Deuteronomy 18 more demanding rather than less. The promise does not open a vague future horizon and then stop. It closes by showing what false prophetic speech looks like and by teaching the hearer not to fear it. That is why the range functions as a real control on the whole cluster.

## Networked Significance
This page strengthens [Prophet Like Moses](../../../../../arguments/prophet-like-moses/summary.md) by making the direct cluster sharper and more accountable. It also helps [Acts 3:22-23](../../../../../bible/nt/acts/ch3/v22-23/summary.md) because Peter's application of Deuteronomy 18 gains weight when the criterion side of the passage is kept in view as well as the promise.

## Contribution Summary
Deuteronomy 18:20-22 strengthens the thesis indirectly but decisively. It does not identify the later figure, but it ensures that the Moses-like office remains serious enough to demand testing, fulfillment, and accountable hearing.
