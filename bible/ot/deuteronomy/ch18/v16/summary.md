# Deuteronomy 18:16

## Page Type
Atomic passage page

## Passage-Specific Contribution
- This verse grounds the promise in Horeb: the people ask not to hear God's voice or see the great fire directly again.
- Its argumentative pressure is mediation, not identification. The prophet promise answers a problem of how divine speech will henceforth be borne.
- It matters because it shows Deuteronomy 18 is about authorized mediated revelation rather than generic inspiration.

## Epistemic Classification
- Status: anchored
- Passage contribution: support within the primary direct range
- Role: Horeb mediation ground for the prophet promise
