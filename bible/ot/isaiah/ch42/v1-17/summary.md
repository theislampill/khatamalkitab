# Isaiah 42:1-17

## Claim Use
This is the main servant-song node in the repository. The working corpus uses the range to argue for a figure who brings law or judgment to the nations, is linked to Kedar, confronts idolatry, and triumphs publicly.

## Why This Range Matters Together
- The servant's mission, law, geography, and victory claims are spread across the range.
- Verse 11 alone is important, but the broader cluster gives it meaning.
- Multiple working files treat the chapter as one integrated argument block.

## Objections / Alternate Readings
- Major alternate readings take the servant as Israel, an idealized servant figure, or Jesus.
- The stronger Arabia-focused arguments depend on how Kedar, Sela, "law," and the anti-idolatry material are handled.
