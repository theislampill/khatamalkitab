# Isaiah 42:1-17

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` supporting passage anchor for the servant/Kedar mission profile. `Contribution:` support; major member of the narrowing complex.

## Claim

This passage is the local center of the servant and Kedar argument. Its importance lies in the fact that servant language, justice, law to the nations, anti-idolatry contrast, new praise, Kedar and Sela references, and victory imagery occur within one passage movement rather than as disconnected proof fragments. That makes the range a major support anchor for the repo's narrowing complex.

## Reasoning

The passage matters because its features accumulate in sequence. The servant is introduced publicly, not privately. Justice and law are pushed outward toward the nations rather than kept within a closed field. The servant does not collapse in weakness but persists until the mission stands. The range then widens into new praise in which Kedar and Sela appear inside the same local movement. The latter part of the passage adds conflict, anti-idolatry pressure, and victory imagery, which keeps the mission public and historical rather than merely devotional.

That sequence is what gives the range its force. If Kedar were detached from the servant profile, the case would become a place-name argument. If the servant profile were detached from Kedar, the case would remain geographically abstract. Read as one unit, the passage prevents both distortions. It shows why servant profile and Kedar-facing material belong to the same local field.

## Local Significance

At local level, Isaiah 42:1-17 shows that the servant profile and the Kedar-facing material belong to one literary and argumentative movement. The page therefore keeps later discussion honest: if Kedar is going to matter, it must matter within a broader servant and mission sequence, not as a detached lexical cue. That local integrity is what gives the page its real value.

## Boundary

The strongest alternatives remain substantial. The servant may be read as Israel, an ideal servant, or Jesus, and the Kedar or Sela language may be read as a praise horizon rather than as candidate narrowing. Those readings keep the passage in support rank. Even so, the range remains one of the repo's most important secondary anchors because it joins public mission-profile material to the Arabian field in a way few other passages do.

## Contribution Summary

Isaiah 42:1-17 strengthens the thesis by supplying the mission-shaped narrowing pressure the cumulative case needs after the primary profile is already in place.
