# Isaiah 60:1-12

`Status:` anchored. `Role:` public-restoration horizon for the widening family. `Contribution:` support for civic and international scale without direct identification.

## Claim
This passage matters because it sets the broader public-restoration horizon within which `vv.6-7` must be read. Nations, kings, gathering, rebuilding, tribute, and open gates give the chapter civic and historical scale. That makes it useful to the widening line even though it is not a direct identification page.

## Reasoning
The range does more than mention prosperity. It describes light drawing nations, kings coming toward restored prominence, sons and daughters gathered, foreigners rebuilding, gates remaining open, and resistant powers judged. That is why the page matters. It shows public prominence, communal restoration, and widened order at chapter scale. It also keeps `vv.6-7` in proportion. The Arabian overlap is real, but it belongs inside a larger Zion-restoration frame rather than governing the chapter by itself.

That framing is crucial to the widening layer. Isaiah 60 is useful not because it supplies another impressive-sounding future scene, but because it describes gathered public life. It imagines communal visibility, ordered access, tribute, rebuilding, and judged resistance. Those are the features that make it relevant to public guidance and public order.

## Local Significance
Locally, Isaiah 60:1-12 gives the widening family a true horizon page. It shows what public consequence looks like when restoration language is allowed to breathe at passage level instead of being reduced to one tribe or one motif.
