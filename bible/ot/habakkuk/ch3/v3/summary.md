# Habakkuk 3:3

## Claim Use
This verse is used as a secondary geography echo linking Teman and Paran to a wider Arabia-oriented fulfillment pattern.

## Why This Passage Gets Its Own Node
- It is cited independently in the working corpus even when Deuteronomy 33 is already in play.
- It often functions as corroboration rather than as the primary anchor.

## Objections / Alternate Readings
- The verse sits in poetic theophany, so direct one-to-one historical mapping is contested.
- Geographic identifications around Teman and Paran remain a major review point.
